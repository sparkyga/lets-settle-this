# Let's Settle This

A daily poll station built for the Latin School of Chicago LED display. 
Students voted by pressing a physical button. The wall updated live.

---

## What it did

A question appeared on the LED display each day. Students walked up and 
pressed one of the physical buttons below the screen to vote. The display 
updated the count in real time and showed a live donut chart tracking the 
split. The previous day's result appeared at the top.

One example: "Hot dogs are sandwiches, not tacos." — 132 to 99.

Questions were submitted by students and organized by the Latin Tech Club. 
No instructions needed. The interaction was designed to be immediate: see 
the question, press a button, watch the wall change.

---

## My role

I was Co-Head of the Tech & Innovation Club and directed this project. My 
work focused on design and UX — how the display was laid out, how the 
voting flow worked from the student's perspective, and how to make the 
station usable in a busy school hallway with no setup time for the user.
